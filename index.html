Student Debt and Young America in 2022

Last Updated December 2022. To see past iterations and analysis, check out the JFI Millennial Student Debt project page.

The nearly 3-year federal student loan repayment pause, in tandem with recent systemic improvements to student loan forgiveness and discharge programs, has precipitated consistent year-over-year decreases in student debt balances between 2020 and 2022. President Biden’s debt cancellation plan will do even more to accelerate the decline. Given the constant growth in borrowing rates and balances over time, the reduction is long-overdue. The consequences of debt relief are widespread and will unfold in the months and years to come—after all, up to 40 million borrowers are eligible for the program and this reprieve will impact their families and communities as well.

In this latest Millennial Student Debt interactive map, we showcase the state-level transformation that is possible when student debt is canceled according to President Biden’s plan. Additionally, we include various important student debt statistics as they stood in June 2022, also available at the county- and ZIP-level. This map is released alongside an in-depth annual report on the student debt crisis in 2022, which includes many additional student debt visualizations and analysis—available here.
